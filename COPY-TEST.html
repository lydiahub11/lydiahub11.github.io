Civil War
In what became known as the War Lord
period, regional factions continued to
battle for power. Eventually, a
Communist supported Kuomintang
(KMT) had been clear to be the de-
facto leading power, but Civil War
would break out after the KMT betrayed
the Communists, massacring them in
theWhiteTerror.
In 1911, after growing rebellion movements,
the Qing Dynasty had fallen, leaving behind a
Chinese Republic that was not to last. With a
President Yuan Shikai continuing to increase
his personal power, reaching the point to
crowning himself Emperor, the Republic fell
into war.
The Chinese
Communist Party
The CCP had gained there first instance of major influence
when they were brought into the KMT; while this may seem
odd, it is worth considering that the KMT themselves had to
rely upon the USSR for support, unable to get any aid from
the west, and so were forced to accept the socialist influence
Cominterm had placed. The CCP themselves, especially after
the events of the Shanghai massacre had established
themselves as an agrarian Stalin-influenced Communist
Party, pledging to end the control of nobles, aristocrats and
the small bourgeois class and place it (through the Party) in
the hands of the People.

USSR
The USSR, a result of the 1917
Communist/Bolshevik Revolution had
established itself as a major power before
the events of the Chinese Civil War and
continued to grow industrially throughout.
While actual aid from the Soviets towards
the Communist party was limited, mostly
due to a "Single State" philosophy held by
Stalin, through both similar ideals and
geographical proximity, the Soviets had
hugely influenced the tactics, beliefs and
actions of Mao Zedong and the Chinese
Communist Party.

The
Nationalist
Party
The KMT had started with an impressive part, forming
as a faction to participate in the overthrowing of the
Qing-Dynasty, establishing their political roots as a
major party in the Chinese Republic. However, with
the growing abuse of power by Yuan Shikai, the KMT
had staged a second revolution, though had failed and
were exiled, struggling to make any major appearance
until the involvement in the War Lord era. The KMT
themselves were a nationalist group who wished to
help the Chinese people through destroying the
feudalistic system. The KMT also controversially had
many fascist influences, a major paramilitary faction
of the party being the Mussolini inspired Blue-Shirts.

East Turkestan Republic
What began as a break away state
during the Civil War, the First East
Turkestan Republic had formed as a
mixture of beliefs from Pan-Turkism
to Islamic freedom, all of which
desiring greater autonomy from
China. With the chaos of the ongoing
Civil War, and the ignorance of the
KMT towards the people's religious
and cultural values, rebellions brew,
leading to the short-lived
organisation of the Republic.

The Fujian Republic
After a faction of the KMT had been sent to
put down Communist rebels in the Fujian

left-wing Fujian Republic. With little
